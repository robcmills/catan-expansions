Modified Placement

Settlements are placed in the center of hexes, not at the corners like in base Catan.

Settlements receive resources from all adjacent hexes (if that resource number is rolled)

No distance rule. Players may build settlements on hexes immediately adjacent to already settled hexes, by same or enemy players.

No roads. Roads are replaced by walls. See Walls section below.

Modified Start

Roll to determine who goes first. In order, players begin by choosing a port and placing one of their ships on that sea hex. Then place one knight on the resource hex adjacent to the port. Then players may place a settlement on the hex occupied by that knight. Optionally, the knight may execute a movement into any adjacent hex and the player may build after the movement.

Once all players have "landed" their ships and placed a knight and a settlement, draw 1 resource for the hex with the starting settlement, and 1 resource for all adjacent hexes to that settlement.

Gold üí∞

Gold hexes are added to the board:

  • Gold hexes produce 1 gold each time their resource number is rolled during a production round
  • 1 gold may be traded to the bank for any 1 resource
  • Gold may be traded with other players

Monopoly card can not be used for gold

Fish Shoals üêü

Fish shoal sea hexes are added to the board:

  • Fish shoal sea hexes produce 1 fish each time their resource number is rolled during a production round
  • 2 fish may be traded to the bank for any 1 resource
  • Fish may be traded with other players

Monopoly card can not be used for fish.

During a 7 production roll (year of plenty for all), each fish shoal produces only 1 fish for each player that occupies it (regardless of how many ships are present).

The Dragon üêâ

For every 7 gold that is produced by gold hexes, the dragon attacks the player with the most points. If more than one player is tied for most points, include knights, ships, current gold and resources in the count.

Dragon attacks occurs immediately after every player has gathered their resources from the production roll.

The victim of the dragon attack must assign a number to each of his settlements and cities and roll a die to determine which one is attacked.

A settlement attacked by the dragon is completely destroyed and removed from the board. A city is downgraded to a settlement, and castles are downgraded to cities.

If the building being attacked is defended by one or more knights, then the player has a chance to fight the dragon. Two dice are rolled, one at a time. The first roll represents the player, the second the dragon. Add +2 to the dragon roll. The higher number wins. Ties are re-rolled.

Storms ‚ö°

Whenever the production roll results in a 12, a violent storm occurs and sinks a ship belonging to the player with the most points.

Storms occur immediately after every player has gathered their resources from the production roll.

The victim of the storm must assign a number to each of his ships (including galleys and cargo ships) and roll a die to determine which one is sunk.

Disease ‚ò£

Whenever the production roll results in a 2, disease kills a knight belonging to the player with the most points.

Disease occurs immediately after every player has gathered their resources from the production roll.

The victim of disease must assign a number to each of his knights (including cavalry and archers) and roll a die to determine which one perishes.

Ships ‚õµ

Players who have built a settlement or city on the coast may build 2 ships or 2 knights or 1 knight and 1 ship per turn

Ship Costs:

  • Ship: 1 wood, 1 sheep
  • Galley: 1 wood, 1 sheep, 1 ore
  • Cargo Ship: 2 wood, 1 sheep

Ship Types:

  • Galley: +1 attack, +1 defense, +1 attack range (limit 1)
  • Cargo Ship: +3 movement, +3 transport, +1 trade, -attack, -defend (limit 3)
    Cargo ships can transport up to 3 knights of any type.
    Cargo ships can not attack or defend other ships or coastal settlements, but they can defend themselves like normal ships.

    Loading and unloading cargo ships:
    Loading knights onto a cargo ship is considered 1 movement that counts against each knight‚Äôs movement range. This means normal knights can only be loaded onto a cargo ship and transported to a different part of the board, but then can not be unloaded on the same turn.
    Cavalry, on the other hand, may continue their movement after being transported by a cargo ship (granted they still have movement points left).
    Archers have a movement range of 1, so like knights they can only be loaded and transported, but because they have an attack range of +1 they could then attack a coastal hex, or enemy ships in the same or adjacent hexes. This makes the combination of a cargo ship loaded with 3 archers a powerful weapon.

Newly built ships are placed on any sea hex adjacent to any of the player's coastal settlements. Ships occupy the center of sea hexes.

On a players turn, each ship may perform 2 actions. Once the player has resolved a ship's actions, lay the piece on its side to denote that it has completed its actions. At the end of the turn, reset all ships to their upright position.

Each action must be unique. A player may not move the same ship twice or attack with the same ship twice.

Ship actions can occur at any point in a player's turn. For example, at the beginning of a player's turn, he may move his ship to a nearby port, and then use that port before building.

Ship actions include the following:

  • Movement: Ships can sail up to 4 sea hexes per movement. (Multiple ships may occupy the same sea hex and ships may sail through sea hexes occupied by another player's ship)
  • Attack: Ships can attack another player's ships if they both occupy the same sea hex. Ships are able to attack knights and settlements on coastal hexes as well. Galleys have powerful cannons (+1 attack range) and can attack ships on adjacent sea hexes. Conversely, only archers can attack a ship from a coastal hex. To resolve an attack, both players involved roll one die and the higher die wins the battle. If attacking a settlement, defender adds 1 to his roll. If attacking a city, defender adds 2 to his roll, castles add 3. A tie must be re-rolled. The results of an attack:
    • If ship to ship attack, victor sinks loser's ship, removes it from the board, and places it in an area near him designated as his ship graveyard. Sunken ships are counted toward the General Victory Token condition.
    • If ship to coastal settlement attack (no defending ships - see below), victor occupies loser's settlement (removes it from the board and replaces it with his own)
    • If the defender wins no action is taken (advantage to attacker)
  • Breach a Sea Wall see Walls section below
  • Fish Roll one die and if the result is 1 or 2, collect 1 fish. 2 Fish may be exchanged for any 1 resource.

Ship benefits: Players who have built ships also may:

  • Defend: When a ship is attacking a coastal settlement, if the defender has a ship on an adjacent sea hex, the attacker must first attack the defending ship. Similarly, regular ships may defend galleys and cargo ships if they are on the same sea hex. A newly built ship may defend on the same turn. Ships can not defend coastal settlements from knight attacks. Defending does not count as an action.
  • Use port trades: Players may use any trade benefits of ports that their ship occupies. (cargo ships get +1)
  • Build: Players may build a new settlement (or knight if playing with knight module) on any coastal hex that is adjacent to one of their ships.

Knights ‚öî

Before the game begins, remove all knight cards from the dev card deck.

Players may build 2 knights or 2 ships or 1 knight and 1 ship per turn

Knight Costs:

  • Knight: 1 ore, 1 sheep
  • Archer: 1 ore, 1 sheep, 1 wood
  • Cavalry: 1 ore, 1 sheep, 1 wheat

Knight Types:

  • Archer: +1 attack range
  • Cavalry: +2 movement

Newly built Knights are placed on any hex settled by the player.

On a players turn, each Knight may perform 2 actions. Once the player has resolved a Knight's actions, lay the piece on its side to denote that it has completed its actions. At the end of the turn, reset all Knights to their upright position.

Each action must be unique. A player may not move the same knight twice or attack with the same knight twice.

Knight actions can occur at any point in a player's turn. For example, at the beginning of a player's turn, he may move his knight to a nearby hex, and then build a settlement there.

Knight actions include the following:

  • Movement Knights can move 1 hex per movement. Multiple knights may occupy the same hex. Knights may move through hexes occupied by another player's knights, however may not move through hexes settled by another player. Knights may move onto an opponents settled hex (to attack) but that ends movement regardless of movement range.
  • Attack Knights can attack another player's knights or settlements if they both occupy the same hex (except for archers, which can attack adjacent hexes). Both players involved roll one die and the higher die wins the battle. If attacking a settlement, defender adds 1 to his roll. If attacking a city, defender adds 2 to his roll, castles add 3. A tie must be re-rolled. The results of an attack:
    • When a knight is defeated in battle the victor captures him and places the piece in an area near the player designated as his prison. Captured knights are counted towards the General Victory Tokens condition.
    • If knight to settlement attack (no defending knights - see below), victor occupies loser's settlement (removes it from the board and replaces it with his own)
    • If the defender wins no action is taken (advantage to attacker)
  • Breach a Wall see Walls section below
  • Hunt Roll one die and if the result is 1 or 2, collect 1 meat. 2 Meat may be exchanged for any 1 resource.

Knight benefits: Players who have built knights also may:

  • Defend: When a knight is attacking a settlement, if the defender has a knight on the hex, that knight is defending that settlement, and the attacker must first attack the defending knight before attacking the settlement. Similarly, knights may defend archers and cavalry. Archers, because of their +1 attack range, may defend settlements on adjacent hexes. During attack phase, a newly built knight may defend on the same turn. Defending does not count as an action.
  • Build: Players may build a new settlement on any unsettled hex that is occupied by one of their knights.

Castles üè∞

Cities may be upgraded to a castle.

Castles produce 3 resources

Walls

Walls are a great way to defend your settlements from attack, however they are not guaranteed to protect you.

Walls may be built on any edge of a player's settled or occupied hexes

Once per a player's turn, he may deconstruct an existing wall and rebuild it in a new location at no cost

Knights may not attack or move through opponent walls, however a player's own knights may move freely or attack through his own walls

Breaching a Wall

  • Players may attempt to breach an opponent's wall if he has a knight occupying an adjacent hex.
  • To breach, roll one die and if the result is a 6, the breach is succesful
  • Upon successful breach of a wall, it is removed from the board and the player may then attack the newly exposed hex if it is occupied or settled
  • Breaching counts as a knight action
  • Breaching may be attempted more than once if the player has more than one knight on adjacent hexes who have not exhausted their actions.
  • Ships may attempt to breach coastal walls

No Robber

The robber is removed from the game entirely. If a 7 is rolled for resource production, this results in a modified "year of plenty" for all players. Each player may collect 1 resource per settlement, 2 resources per city, and 3 per castle they have built. Players must choose which resources to collect from the adjacent resources to each settlement/city/castle. Fish shoals produce 1 fish for each player that occupies it with at least one ship.

There is no penalty for having more than 7 cards at any time

Game Phases

  • Phase 1 - Build: During this phase no attack actions are allowed.
  • Phase 2 - Attack: During this phase attack actions are allowed and turn order is modified:

    Attack phase turn order: At the start of each round, calculate each player's score by summing their points (including knights and ships). The player with the lowest score goes first, highest last. Recalculate at the start of each round.

Each phase lasts 3 rounds. Implement a counter to keep track.

Rewards

  • If any player does not receive resources on a production roll, he may draw one gold.
  • At the end of each round, the player with the least points may draw one free dev card.

New Dev Cards

  • Loaded Dice: Player may choose the outcome of the resource production roll.
  • Relic: You have acquired a rare religious relic of supernatural power. Grant this valuable item to the knight of your choice and add 1 to all his battle rolls for the remainder of the game. When hunting a 3 also results in a successful hunt. (place a chit underneath the knight to mark this ability)
  • Liquid Fire: Your alchemist has discovered an incendiary formula that can be used in naval warfare to great effect because it ignites upon contact with water. Grant this valuable weapon to the ship of your choice and add 1 to all its battle rolls for the remainder of the game. (place a chit underneath the ship to mark this ability)
  • Gemstones: You unearth a deposit of rare minerals that fetch a high price at market. Collect 4 gold pieces.
  • Robin Hood Robbers: A band of benevolent pirates steals from the leader and gives to the loser. The player with the most points must give half his resources to the player with the least points. Resolve this action immediately.

Points

Points are used during the game, but do not determine the winner. For victory conditions, see the Victory Tokens section.

  • Building: 1 point per wall, 1 point per settlement, 2 per city, 3 per castle
  • Armies and Navies: 1 point per ship, 2 points per galley/cargo ship, 1 point per knight, 2 points per archer/cavalry

Victory Tokens

At the end of the game, the victor is the player with the most victory tokens.

  • Conqueror: 1 token for each settlement that you conquer from another player. 2 tokens for cities, 3 for castles
  • Siege: 1 token for each wall breached
  • Fortress: 1 token for completely enclosing one or more settlements with walls.
  • Great Wall: 1 token for building a wall of length 5 or more
  • Attacker: 1 token for each battle in which you emerge victorious as the attacker. This means clearing a hex of more than 1 enemy.
  • Defender: 1 token for each battle in which you successfully survive an attacking enemy with a larger force.
  • Traveler: 1 token for each ship that moves more than 6 sea hexes away from its home port and 1 token for each knight that travels more than 3 hexes away from its home settlement
  • Trader: 1 token for trading at least 1 resource with every player on one turn
  • Armada: 1 token for a standing navy of 5 or more ships (once per player)
  • Legion: 1 token for a standing army of 5 or more knights (once per player)
  • Metropolis: 1 token for building 3 or more settlements on adjacent hexes
  • Balance: At the end of each round the leader must give 1 of his tokens to the player with the least points. If the leader has none, draw from supply.

At the end of the game:

  • Monarch: 3 tokens for each island that you have settled and no other player has settled or occupied.
  • General: 3 tokens for the player with the most sunken ships and prisoners. If a tie exists both players receive tokens.
  • Colonist: 3 tokens for each island that you have settled that is not the starting island. These settlements must still belong to the player at the end of the game.
  • Builder: 3 tokens for the player with the highest score
