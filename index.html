This is a collection of expansions to the game of Catan. All rules of the base game apply with the following new rules:

Accelerated Start

First placement is settlement, second city. Then draw 1 resource for each hex adjacent to settlement and 2 resources for each hex adjacent to city.

Gold üí∞

Gold hexes are added to the board and operate much the same as they do in the Explorers and Pirates Expansion:

  • Gold hexes produce gold each time their resource number is rolled during a production round
  • 2 gold may be traded to the bank for any 1 resource
  • Gold may be traded with other players

In a 4 player game, with a base Catan board, Gold hexes replace 1 wheat, 1 sheep and 1 wood hex resulting in a balanced 3 hexes per resource plus 1 desert board configuration

Monopoly card can not be used for gold

The Dragon üêâ

For every 7 gold that is produced by gold hexes, the dragon attacks the player with the most victory points. If more than one player is tied for most victory points, include knights, ships, current gold and resources in the count.

Dragon attacks occurs immediately after every player has gathered their resources from the production roll.

The victim of the dragon attack must assign a number to each of his settlements and cities and roll a die to determine which one is attacked.

A settlement attacked by the dragon is completely destroyed and removed from the board. A city is downgraded to a settlement, and castles are downgraded to cities.

If the building being attacked is defended by one or more knights, then the player has a chance to fight the dragon. Two dice are rolled, one at a time. The first roll represents the player, the second the dragon. The higher number wins. Ties are re-rolled.

Ships ‚õµ

Players who have built a settlement or city on the coast may build 1 ship per turn

If playing with knights, players may build 2 knights or 2 ships or 1 knight and 1 ship per turn

Ship Costs:

  • Ship: 1 wood, 1 wool
  • War Ship: 1 wood, 1 wool, 1 ore
  • Cargo Ship: 2 wood, 1 wool

Ship Types:

  • War Ship: +2 attack, +2 defense (limit 1)
  • Cargo Ship: +3 movement, ability to carry up to 3 knights, +1 trade (limit 3)

Newly built ships are placed on any sea hex adjacent to any of the player's coastal settlements. Ships occupy the center of sea hexes, not corners like settlements do. Place them as you would resource number tokens.

On a players turn, each ship may perform 2 actions. Once the player has resolved a ship's actions, lay the piece on its side to denote that it has completed its actions. At the end of the turn, reset all ships to their upright position.

Building a ship is considered an action, so a player may build and move, or build and attack (if in attack phase) but may not build, move, and attack with the same ship on the same turn.

Each action must be unique. A player may not move the same ship twice or attack with the same ship twice.

Ship actions can occur at any point in a player's turn. For example, at the beginning of a player's turn, he may move his ship to a nearby port, and then use that port before building.

Ship actions include the following:

  • Movement: Ships can sail up to 4 sea hexes per movement. (Multiple ships may occupy the same sea hex and ships may sail through sea hexes occupied by another player's ship)
  • Attack: Ships can attack another player's ships or coastal settlements if they both occupy the same sea hex. Both players involved roll one die and the higher die wins the battle. If attacking a settlement, defender adds 1 to his roll. If attacking a city, defender adds 2 to his roll. A tie must be re-rolled. The results of an attack:
    • If ship to ship attack, victor sinks loser's ship and removes it from the board
    • If ship to coastal settlement attack (no defending ships - see below), victor occupies loser's settlement (removes it from the board and replaces it with his own)
    • If the defender wins no action is taken (advantage to attacker)

Ship benefits: Players who have built ships also may:

  • Defend: When a ship is attacking a coastal settlement, war or cargo ship, if the defender has a ship on an adjacent sea hex, the attacker must first attack the defending ship. A newly built ship may defend on the same turn. Ships can not defend coastal settlements from knight attacks. Defending does not count as an action.
  • Use port trades: Players may use any trade benefits of ports that their ship occupies
  • Build: Players may build a new settlement (or knight if playing with knight module) on any coastal hex that is adjacent to one of their ships (respecting distance rule)
  • Largest Navy: The first player to build 3 ships receives the Largest Navy card that is worth 2 victory points. Like Largest Army, this may be stolen if another player builds a larger Navy, or the size of their fleet is reduced by attack.

Knights

Before the game begins, remove all knight cards from the dev card deck. During the trade and build phase of a player's turn, he may build 1 knight per turn

If playing with ships, players may build 2 knights or 2 ships or 1 knight and 1 ship per turn

Knight Costs:

  • Knight: 1 ore, 1 wool
  • Archer: 1 ore, 1 wool, 1 wood
  • Cavalry: 1 ore, 1 wool, 1 wheat

Knight Types:

  • Archer: +1 attack range
  • Cavalry: +2 movement

Newly built Knights are placed on any hex adjacent to any of the player's settlements. Knights occupy the center of hexes, not corners like settlements do. Place them as you would resource number tokens.

On a players turn, each Knight may perform 2 actions. Once the player has resolved a Knight's actions, lay the piece on its side to denote that it has completed its actions. At the end of the turn, reset all Knights to their upright position.

Building a knight is considered an action, so a player may build and move, or build and attack (if in attack phase) but may not build, move, and attack with the same knight on the same turn.

Each action must be unique. A player may not move the same knight twice or attack with the same knight twice.

Knight actions can occur at any point in a player's turn. For example, at the beginning of a player's turn, he may move his knight to a nearby hex, and then build a settlement there (respecting distance rule).

Knight actions include the following:

  • Movement Knights can move 1 hex across unsettled territory, or two hexes that are adjacent to roads, per movement (multiple knights may occupy the same hex and knights may move through hexes occupied by another player's knights)
  • Attack Knights can attack another player's knights or settlements if they both occupy the same hex. Both players involved roll one die and the higher die wins the battle. If attacking a settlement, defender adds 1 to his roll. If attacking a city, defender adds 2 to his roll. A tie must be re-rolled. The results of an attack:
    • If knight to knight attack, victor kills loser's knight and removes it from the board
    • If knight to settlement attack (no defending knights - see below), victor occupies loser's settlement (removes it from the board and replaces it with his own)
    • If the defender wins no action is taken (advantage to attacker)

Knight benefits: Players who have built knights also may:

  • Defend: When a knight is attacking a settlement, if the defender has a knight on an adjacent hex, that knight is defending that settlement, and the attacker must first attack the defending knight before attacking the settlement. During attack phase, a newly built knight may defend on the same turn. Knights can not defend a settlement from a ship attack. Defending does not count as an action.
  • Build: Players may build a new settlement (or ship if playing with ship module) on any hex that is occupied by one of their knights (respecting distance rule)
  • Largest Army: This rule applies the same as base Catan. The first player to build 3 knights get Largest Army which is worth 2 victory points. Another player may steal if he builds a larger army, or a player's army is reduced by attack.

Castles

Cities may be upgraded to a castle.

Castles are built with high walls that are easily defended, thus making them immune to attack by another player's knights.

Castles are not immune to dragon attacks

Castles produce 3 resources

Castles cost 1 ore, 1 wheat, 1 sheep, 1 wood, 1 brick

No Robber

The robber is removed from the game entirely. If a 7 is rolled for resource production, this results in a modified "year of plenty" for all players (except during Attack phase - described below). Each player may collect 1 resource per settlement and 2 resources per city they have built. Players must choose which resources to collect from the adjacent resources to each settlement/city.

There is no penalty for having more than 7 cards at any time

Turn Order

Roll the dice to determine initial placement and turn order for the first round. Place a "first" marker in front of the player to go first. Turns are executed to the left, counter-clockwise around the table until play returns to the first player. After every player has taken a turn, the "first" marker is transfered to left, and that player goes first for the next round, and the former first player becomes the last to take his turn in the next round.

Resource production rolls occur at the beginning of each players turn.

Game Phases

If you play with knights and ships and elect to grant them destructive powers, this can greatly lengthen the game. To remedy this, and also to keep the game to a reasonable length, you may elect to play the game with three phases:

  • Phase 1 - Build: During this phase, players may only build. No attack actions are allowed.
  • Phase 2 - Attack: During this phase attack actions are allowed and turn order is modified:

    Attack phase turn order: Calculate each player's score by summing their victory points (including knights and ships). The player with the lowest score goes first, highest last.

The number of rounds in each phase is equal to the number of players. In a four player game, each phase lasts 4 rounds. In a 5 player game each phase lasts 5 rounds. Implement a counter to keep track. At the end of the last round, the player with the most victory points wins. If a tie exists declare more than one winner.

Victory Points

To encourage a variety of strategies, victory points can be earned several ways:

  • Building: 1 point per settlement, 2 per city, 3 per castle
  • Largest Army/Navy: 2 points
  • Largest Metropolis: 2 points - largest metropolis is counted as most points from buildings that are adjacent or joined by roads
  • Longest Road: 2 points
  • Colonist: 1 point per island settled that is not settled on initial placement
  • Harbor Master: 2 points
